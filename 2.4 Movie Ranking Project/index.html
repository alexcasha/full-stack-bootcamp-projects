The Best Movies According to Alex

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire holds a special place in my heart as it is the film where the wizarding world grows darker, richer, and more complex. One of the most captivating aspects of this movie is that it's the first time we truly encounter Lord Voldemort’s crew, the Death Eaters, in action. Their ominous presence adds a chilling depth to the story, marking a significant turning point in the series.

This is also the movie where we witness Lord Voldemort himself returning to physical form. Ralph Fiennes’ portrayal brings a terrifying and powerful presence to the screen, making Voldemort more than just a name. For me, it’s the first movie that delves deeper into his character, showing his cunning, cruelty, and influence, which sets the stage for the darker battles to come.

Additionally, the film is a spectacle of special effects, from the breathtaking Triwizard Tournament scenes to the haunting resurrection of Voldemort in the graveyard. Each scene is visually stunning and filled with tension, making it impossible to look away.

The combination of expanding the lore around the series’ ultimate villain and pushing the boundaries of magical effects makes The Goblet of Fire an unforgettable chapter in the Harry Potter saga and my personal favorite.

The Proposal

The Proposal is the perfect blend of humor, charm, and heartfelt moments, making it one of my favorite romantic comedies. The movie takes the classic enemies-to-lovers trope and gives it a fresh, hilarious twist with unforgettable performances by Sandra Bullock and Ryan Reynolds.

What sets this movie apart is its unique premise. Margaret Tate, a high-powered, no-nonsense editor, is forced to fake an engagement with her assistant, Andrew Paxton, to avoid deportation. Their dynamic is electric, full of witty banter and moments of vulnerability that gradually reveal their deeper layers.

The chemistry between Bullock and Reynolds is undeniable. They perfectly balance comedy with emotional depth, making their journey from reluctant co-conspirators to something more feel natural and heartwarming.

The backdrop of Andrew’s quirky, tight-knit family in Alaska adds another layer of charm. From Betty White’s delightful antics as Grandma Annie to the breathtaking scenery, these elements create a cozy, endearing atmosphere that makes you want to revisit the film again and again.

What I love most about The Proposal is how it combines laugh-out-loud humor with a genuine story of growth and connection. It’s more than just a rom-com—it’s a reminder that love can surprise you in the most unexpected ways.

Maze Runner

The Maze Runner is one of those films that grabs you from the very first moment and doesn’t let go. From its unique premise to its intense pacing, it’s a movie that stands out in the dystopian genre.

What I love most is the sense of mystery and urgency. The story begins with Thomas waking up in the Glade, surrounded by towering walls and a maze that seems impossible to escape. Like Thomas, we’re thrown into this unknown world with no memory of how we got there, making every twist and revelation feel personal and thrilling.

The movie’s cast brings the story to life with a mix of strength and vulnerability. Dylan O’Brien shines as Thomas, capturing the perfect balance of determination and curiosity. The camaraderie and conflict among the Gladers—especially with characters like Newt, Minho, and Gally—add layers of tension and emotional depth to the story.

The maze itself is a character in its own right. The design, the dynamic shifts, and the terrifying Grievers create a sense of danger that keeps you on edge throughout the movie. The special effects and cinematography make the maze feel alive, as if it’s constantly watching and challenging the Gladers.

What makes The Maze Runner so compelling is how it blends action, mystery, and character development. It’s not just about survival—it’s about unraveling the bigger picture behind the maze, WCKD, and what’s really at stake. The unanswered questions and cliffhanger ending leave you desperate for more, making it the perfect start to an unforgettable series.
